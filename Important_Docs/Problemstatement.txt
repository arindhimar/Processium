Organizations face significant challenges in managing recruitment workflows due to the absence of a structured, reusable, and governance-driven hiring framework. Existing Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) primarily focus on candidate data management rather than process orchestration, resulting in fragmented communication, unclear accountability, and inconsistent evaluation standards across departments.

There is a need for a configurable recruitment workflow engine that enables organizations to:
Design standardized hiring templates
Assign structured roles using RACI matrices
Define conditional hiring steps
Track ownership and accountability
Maintain audit trails for compliance
Reuse and version control hiring structures

Without such a system, recruitment remains reactive, manual, and prone to inefficiencies, especially in organizations with multiple stakeholders such as HR, finance, technical teams, and compliance units.

Enterprise-Level Problem Framing
Large and mid-scale organizations require:
Multi-department coordination
Role-based accountability
SLA monitoring
Compliance tracking
Audit visibility

However, most hiring workflows are informal and not system-enforced.

This leads to:
Delayed offers
Offer rejections
Budget approval conflicts
Legal risks
Poor hiring quality

There is currently no unified platform that treats recruitment as a governed workflow system rather than just a candidate tracking pipeline.
